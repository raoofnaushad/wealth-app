## Business Description

Envirosoil NV (trading as Vysoil) is a Belgian environmental consultancy and remediation firm founded in 1992, headquartered in Oostkamp, West Flanders. The company operates across all three Belgian regions and serves public authorities, industrial operators, construction firms, engineering offices, and private individuals.

**Service Lines:**
- **Soil investigation:** indicative surveys (orienterend onderzoek), delineation studies (afperkend onderzoek), risk analysis, human-health assessments, confirmatory investigations, mandatory pre-transaction soil reports
- **Remediation design and execution:** remediation design (saneringsontwerp), supervised on-site execution, in-situ and ex-situ treatment techniques, emergency response / calamity management, second opinions on third-party remediation plans
- **Drilling and in-situ techniques:** in-house drilling and soil sampling teams (core operational differentiator), MIP (Membrane Interface Probe) scanning, groundwater sampling and long-term monitoring, pump tests, CPT and geomechanical characterisation
- **Waste and secondary materials:** demolition inventories (sloopopvolgingsplan), asbestos surveys and asbestos inventory certification, granulates and aggregate classification, soil movement tracking (GRONDBANK), circular economy advisory
- **Water body and sediment:** aquatic sediment investigation, water body contamination assessment, dredging supervision
- **Project coordination and advisory:** full dossier management with OVAM / BIM / SPAQuE, permitting support and regulatory liaison, technical review of external environmental reports

**Business Model:** Capital-light, cash-generative, project-based revenue streams. Diversified demand driven by mandatory compliance work. Pricing discipline on complex projects achieves consistent margins. In-house field teams (drilling, sampling) are a core differentiator vs. advisory-only competitors — they eliminate subcontractor dependency on high-margin technical fieldwork.

## Regulatory and Licensing Position (Primary Structural Moat)

Envirosoil holds a comprehensive suite of regional accreditations across all three Belgian regions. This multi-jurisdictional licensing stack is the primary structural moat of the business.

**Accreditation Register:**
- **OVAM-recognised soil remediation expert (Types I and II):** Issued by OVAM (Flemish Public Waste Agency) — soil investigation and remediation design, Flanders
- **BIM accreditation:** Issued by Brussels Institute for Environmental Management — environmental consultancy and soil operations, Brussels region
- **SPAQuE certification:** Issued by SPAQuE — soil investigation and remediation operations, Walloon region
- **Asbestos inventory certification:** Issued by OVAM — asbestos surveys and inventories, Belgium-wide
- **CRODIS certification:** Certified operator for waste and soil movement tracking, Belgium-wide
- **GRONDBANK certification:** Soil movement tracking and construction/demolition oversight, Belgium-wide
- **ISO-aligned internal quality management procedures:** All operations

**Barrier-to-Entry Assessment:**
- OVAM accreditation is a hard prerequisite — operating as a soil remediation expert in Flanders requires OVAM recognition; this demands demonstrated technical competency and cannot be simply purchased
- Multi-region licensing is a differentiator — most smaller Belgian operators are single-region licensed; holding OVAM + BIM + SPAQuE simultaneously is rare
- 30-year regulatory stringency trend — Belgian soil regulation under the Flemish Soil Decree (Bodemdecreet) has consistently trended toward greater stringency, raising the compliance bar for new entrants
- In-house field capability moat — proprietary drilling rigs and sampling equipment represent a capital and operational barrier that pure advisory firms cannot replicate
- Circular economy regulatory expansion — CRODIS and GRONDBANK certifications position for emerging mandatory compliance in construction/demolition waste

**Key Diligence Points on Accreditation:**
- Missing: accreditation expiry dates and renewal conditions for all seven certifications
- Unresolved: whether OVAM accreditation is entity-held or individual-held (in Belgian practice, erkend bodemsaneringsdeskundige status is typically tied to named individuals — if co-founders hold personal accreditation, their departure triggers a regulatory compliance crisis)
- Not addressed: whether accreditations are transferable in an M&A transaction or whether change-of-control triggers re-application
- Wallonia and Brussels active coverage not substantiated with specific licence numbers

## Market Context and Demand Drivers

**Market Size:** Estimated EUR 600M+ annual Belgian environmental services market, growing at approximately 8% CAGR driven by regulatory expansion. Approximately 100,000+ potentially contaminated sites across Belgium representing a structural, multi-decade remediation backlog.

**Legislative / Regulatory-Mandated Demand (Non-Discretionary, independent of economic cycles):**
- **Flemish Soil Decree (Bodemdecreet):** Mandates soil investigation and remediation upon every property transfer, change of use, or discovery of contamination — creating a legally enforceable demand floor for every real estate transaction in Flanders
- **Brussels equivalent (BIM):** Equivalent mandatory investigation requirements for Brussels
- **Walloon equivalent (SPAQuE):** Statutory investigation and remediation obligations for Wallonia
- **Statutory remediation timelines:** Legal deadlines imposed on landowners and industrial operators
- **PFAS regulations:** Expanded mandatory survey requirements from emerging PFAS contamination standards
- **Asbestos survey mandates:** Mandatory inventory requirements prior to demolition or renovation
- **Circular economy regulations (GRONDBANK, CRODIS):** Certified oversight requirements for construction/demolition projects
- **EU Environmental Liability Directive (2004/35/EC):** Mandatory remediation obligations on industrial operators at EU level

**Discretionary / Commercial Demand (cyclical):**
- Construction and real estate due diligence (correlated with transaction volumes)
- Energy transition site decommissioning
- Voluntary circular economy advisory
- Second opinions on third-party remediation plans

**Key Diligence Points on Market:**
- No legislative vs. discretionary revenue split provided — if 40%+ is construction-linked, cyclical risk is materially higher than presented
- EUR 600M TAM and 8% CAGR figures are CIM-stated and unsourced — no independent verification
- Construction-cycle exposure risk is unquantified

## IC Recommendation (from Investment Memorandum)

**Overall IC Stance: WATCHLIST — DO NOT PROCEED TO LOI**

The business presents a structurally coherent thesis anchored in regulatory-mandated demand and a credible accreditation moat. However, the CIM contains multiple material omissions and at least three unexplained financial anomalies.

**What the Materials Support (Block A):**
- Regulatory moat via Belgian Soil Decree (non-discretionary demand floor)
- Seven distinct accreditations across all Belgian regions (barrier to entry)
- FY2024 EBITDA margin of 18.4% at upper end of peer range

- Founder continuity post-LBO
- Buy-and-build logic in fragmented market with A+E Consult proof-of-concept
- In-house field capability moat
- EUR 600M+ market with ~8% CAGR

**What Remains Unresolved (Block B — 10 material open items):**
1. No entry price, valuation, or return profile disclosed (most critical omission)
2. Revenue stagnation: exactly EUR 10,359K in three consecutive years (statistically implausible)
3. FY2024 balance sheet collapse: -39% assets, implied EUR 3,079K pre-sale distribution
4. FY2022 margin compression (-790bps) unexplained
5. Accreditation transferability unconfirmed (entity vs. individual level)
6. No founder retention structure (equity rollover, lock-up, non-compete)
7. A+E Consult terms, financials, integration plan all undisclosed
8. No revenue breakdown by service line or demand trigger
9. Client concentration undisclosed
10. No second-tier management identified

**Conditions for Positive IC Decision:**
- **Critical:** Seller provides asking price; audited accounts FY2022-2024; FY2024 equity decline explained; OVAM entity-level accreditation confirmed; binding founder retention agreements
- **High:** Revenue breakdown by demand trigger; A+E Consult full disclosure; client concentration analysis; FY2022 margin compression explained
